# 6. HOW IT WORKS — The Call Journey

Here is what happens when a patient calls, step by step. **You do nothing.**

### Step 1: Patient calls (0 seconds)
The patient dials the AI number. It is a local number your boss chooses.

### Step 2: AI answers (2 seconds)
The AI responds with a natural voice: *"Hello, [Your Business], I am your virtual assistant. How may I help you?"*

### Step 3: Conversation (30 seconds – 2 minutes)
The patient says what they need. The AI understands and reacts:
- **"I want an appointment"** → AI offers available slots, confirms date and time
- **"What are your rates?"** → AI gives indicative rates
- **"I have an emergency"** → AI gives emergency procedure and transfers if needed
- **"I speak Spanish"** → AI switches to Spanish instantly

### Step 4: Appointment created (3 seconds after hang-up)
The moment the patient hangs up, the AI sends the appointment to Google Calendar. The patient receives a confirmation text.

### Step 5: You see the result (immediate)
- Your Google Calendar shows: "APT — [patient number] — [time]"
- Patient receives: *"Your appointment is confirmed..."*
- Your dashboard shows: "Call received at 10:32 — APT confirmed — Duration 1min 45s"

**You did nothing. The appointment is booked. The patient is happy. You just verify.**

# 11. FAQ — Questions You Are Asking

**1. Will the AI replace me?**  
→ **No.** The AI is your **assistant**. It handles repetitive calls and off-hours (night, break, vacation). **You stay in your role** and focus on high-value tasks: in-person greeting, file management, premium patient relations. You gain productivity and peace of mind.

**2. What if the AI doesn't understand a patient?**  
→ It politely asks to repeat. If it truly doesn't understand, it offers to take a message or transfer to a human. **You can listen to transcripts in the dashboard** and call the patient back if needed.

**3. Will patients know it's an AI?**  
→ The AI does not say it. It introduces itself as "the assistant at [Your Business]." The voice is natural. Most patients don't notice, especially for short calls. And even if they guess, **they prefer an AI that answers over a human who doesn't pick up.**

**4. Can I change hours or services later?**  
→ Yes. Within the 30-day support period, the developer modifies the prompt for free (1 time). After that, it is billed at $100/hour. But you can also learn to do it yourself — the developer trains you.

**5. What if we have no calls one month?**  
→ You only pay the Twilio number (~$1-3). Vapi only bills for minutes used. No surprises.

**6. Can the AI handle emergencies?**  
→ Yes. It is programmed to recognize emergency keywords. It gives the procedure and transfers to your mobile if needed.

**7. Is my data secure?**  
→ Yes. Webhooks are cryptographically signed. Data stays on your server. The developer does not resell anything. You own all accounts.

**8. Can I have multiple numbers?**  
→ Yes, but each additional number = a new billed setup.

**9. What if Vapi or Twilio raises prices?**

**10. How do I stop the service?**
